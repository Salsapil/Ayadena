History of Pottery

Egyptian pottery, dating back to around 4,000 BCE, played a crucial role in the daily and ceremonial life of ancient Egypt. Initially crafted from Nile silt, these early pots were hand-shaped and fired in simple kilns. As techniques advanced, potters began using the wheel, allowing for more uniform and intricate designs. Egyptian pottery ranged from utilitarian wares, such as storage jars and cooking pots, to finely decorated ceremonial pieces and funerary items.
